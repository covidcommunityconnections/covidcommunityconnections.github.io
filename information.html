COVID Fact Checker

Below is a list of common misconceptions about COVID-19 with links to the correct facts related to the statement.

Myth: The flu shot will protect you against the coronavirus.
Fact: “If you have received a flu vaccine, it will not protect against coronaviruses, but will help prevent the flu. Getting the flu could make you more vulnerable to other infections. Getting the flu vaccine will not increase your risk of illness from coronavirus.” (Government of Canada)
Read More: Oxford Academic

Myth: If you can hold your breath for 10 seconds without coughing or having discomfort, you do not have COVID-19 or any other lung disease .
Fact: COVID-19 can not be confirmed with these breathing exercises, and can only truly be confirmed by getting a laboratory test done.
Read More: Canadian Digestive Health Foundation

Myth: If I am a young and healthy adult, I cannot get infected by COVID-19.
Fact: People of all ages can be infected by COVID-19 and should be following good hand hygiene and social distance. It is true, however, that older people and people with pre-existing medical conditions are more vulnerable to becoming severely ill.
Read More: Canadian Digestive Health Foundation

Myth: By consuming certain foods, I will boost my immune system so I can better prevent catching COVID-19.
Fact: There is no magical food or vitamin that can prevent someone from catching COVID-19. The best preventative measures include good hygiene and social distancing.
Read More: Canadian Digestive Health Foundation

Myth: UV lights can be used to disinfect your skin.
Fact: UV lamps should not be used for this and they can irritate and damage your skin and eyes. The most effective ways for cleaning are using water and soap to wash your hands and using alcohol-based hand rub.
Read More: WHO

Myth: Rinsing your nose with saline every day prevents infection with COVID-19.
Fact: There is currently no evidence that regularly rinsing your nose with saline can protect people from COVID-19.
Read More: WHO

Myth: Hydroxychloroquine can treat COVID-19.
Fact: Currently, there is no data proving that hydroxychloroquine can treat or reduce mortality rates of COVID-19 patients.
Read More: WHO

Myth: Warm weather and the sun protect you from COVID-19.
Fact: You can catch COVID-19, no matter the weather. The best way to protect yourself from COVID-19 is to practice proper hygiene and social distancing.
Read More: WHO
